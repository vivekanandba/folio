# Flexi-cap vs multi-cap

> Category names sell a promise. Rules decide what managers *must* do. Freedom decides what they *can* ignore.

## Big idea

In Indian mutual funds, **multi-cap** and **flexi-cap** sound similar but are built under different SEBI rules. Multi-cap funds must keep floors across large, mid, and small caps. Flexi-cap funds only need a high equity floor and may roam the capitalisation spectrum.

## Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Multi-cap | Diversified equity with **minimum 25% each** in large, mid, and small caps (post-2020 redesign) |
| Flexi-cap | Diversified equity with **≥65% equity**; no mandated mid/small floors |
| Style | Where the portfolio actually sits (e.g. large-cap heavy) vs what the label suggests |

## Why it matters

If you buy “go anywhere” and get “mostly large caps forever,” you may be paying active fees for an experience closer to a large-cap fund — with different return path than a true multi-cap exposure.

## Remember

- Label = marketing + regulatory bucket
- Holdings = what you actually own
- Check allocation periodically, not just category name
