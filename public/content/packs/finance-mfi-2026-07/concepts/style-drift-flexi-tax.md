> Freedom that is never used is not free — it has a cost you only notice later.

## Big idea

When flexi-cap funds keep a large-cap-heavy mix through crashes and recoveries, investors can miss the mid/small-led phases that a freer mandate was meant to capture. That quiet gap — paying for flexibility you do not get — is the informal **“flexi-cap tax.”**

## How the story started (compressed)

1. Multi-caps were criticised for drifting into large caps.
2. SEBI forced multi-caps toward true three-bucket floors.
3. Flexi-cap arrived as the go-anywhere alternative without those floors.
4. Many flexi-caps still settled back toward large caps.

## Trade-off (intuition)

Large-cap weight can cushion drawdowns a little, while quieter mid/small exposure can lag in recoveries and long SIP stretches when smaller names lead. Over decades, small annual gaps compound into very different corpuses.

## Investor move

- Know what **your** fund owns today (large / mid / small mix).
- Separate *reading a finding* from *restless churning*.
- Prefer patient course-correction on fresh investments over tax-blind exits.
