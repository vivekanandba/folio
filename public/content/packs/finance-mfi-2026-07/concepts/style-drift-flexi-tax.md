# Style drift & the "flexi-cap tax"

> Freedom that is never used is not free — it has a cost you only notice later.

> [!key] The "flexi-cap tax" is the quiet gap you pay when a go-anywhere fund parks in large caps and never uses its freedom — you carry active fees and miss the mid/small-led phases the mandate was sold to capture.

## Big idea

When flexi-cap funds keep a large-cap-heavy mix through crashes *and* recoveries, investors can miss the mid/small-led runs that a freer mandate was meant to catch. Paying for flexibility you never receive is the informal **flexi-cap tax**.

## The trade-off (intuition)

Large-cap weight can cushion drawdowns a little; quieter mid/small exposure can lag in recoveries and long SIP stretches when smaller names lead. **Over decades, small annual gaps compound into very different corpuses** — the tax is invisible year to year and large in the end.

> [!more] Why "compounding small gaps" is the whole point
> A 1.5% annual shortfall sounds tiny. On a 20-year SIP it can be the difference between comfortably hitting a goal and missing it. See [expense ratio & TER](#/pack/finance-mfi-2026-07/concept/expense-ratio-ter) for the same compounding-drag maths applied to fees.

## Investor move

- Know what **your** fund owns *today* (large / mid / small mix).
- Separate **reading a finding** from **restless churning**.
- Prefer patient course-correction on *fresh* investments over tax-blind exits that trigger real capital-gains tax.

> [!warn] Selling to "fix" style drift can cost more in capital-gains tax than the drift itself. Redirect new money before you disturb old money.
