# Active share

> Being different from the index is a *process* claim — not a performance guarantee.

## Big idea

**Active share** measures how different a fund’s portfolio is from its benchmark. Roughly: add absolute weight differences across securities and divide by two.

`Active share ≈ ½ × Σ |w_fund − w_index|`

- ~80% → largely different from the index  
- ~10% → behaves a lot like an index fund  

## What it does *not* mean

Higher active share does **not** reliably imply higher outperformance. Extremely active funds can form a binary set: some consistently beat, others consistently fail — divergence measures intention and risk, not skill.

## Investor move

- Ask: am I paying active fees for low active share?
- Ask separately: has active risk been rewarded over full cycles?
- Do not treat “most active” lists as “best funds” lists.
