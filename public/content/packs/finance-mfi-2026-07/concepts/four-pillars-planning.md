# Four pillars of planning

> A portfolio of SIPs is not the same thing as a plan.

## Big idea

Wealth creation alone does not equal financial security. A workable plan balances **Protection**, **Growth**, **Liquidity**, and **Goals**.

## The four pillars

| Pillar | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Protection | Life and health cover against income loss or medical shocks |
| Growth | Long-term investments for wealth creation |
| Liquidity | Emergency / near-term cash so you do not break long-term assets |
| Goals | Named outcomes (education, retirement, house) with rough timelines |

## Common imbalance

Many people over-index on **Growth** (SIPs, equity) while employer health cover, thin life insurance, and no contingency fund leave the household fragile under a health scare or income blip.

## Key takeaways

- Investments create wealth; they do not replace insurance and cash buffers.
- Insurance is one piece of protection — not the whole plan.
- Emergency liquidity is as critical as long-term growth.
- Map goals explicitly; do not assume “portfolio = plan.”
