# Four pillars of planning

> A portfolio of SIPs is not the same thing as a plan.

> [!key] Wealth creation ≠ financial security. A real plan stands on four pillars — **Protection, Liquidity, Goals, Growth** — and most people over-build the last one while the first two stay thin.

## Big idea

A plan balances four things. Growth alone leaves a household fragile: one health scare or income gap and the long-term SIPs get liquidated at the worst time.

## The four pillars

| Pillar | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Protection | Life and health cover against income loss or medical shocks |
| Liquidity | Emergency / near-term cash so you don't break long-term assets |
| Goals | Named outcomes (education, retirement, house) with rough timelines |
| Growth | Long-term investments for wealth creation |

## Sequence matters

> [!tip] Build the pillars in order of fragility, not excitement: **Protection → Liquidity → Goals → Growth.** Insurance and a cash buffer come *before* scaling SIPs, because they're what stop you from selling growth assets under stress.

> [!more] Why protection comes first
> Growth assets are volatile precisely when you're most likely to need cash (job loss, medical events often cluster with downturns). Without protection and liquidity, a shock forces a sale at a low — converting a paper dip into a permanent loss. The first two pillars exist to protect the fourth.

## Key takeaways

- Investments create wealth; they don't replace insurance and cash buffers.
- Insurance is one piece of protection — not the whole plan.
- Emergency liquidity is as critical as long-term growth.
- Map goals explicitly; don't assume "portfolio = plan."
