# Crowded corners

> When many funds and many cities chase the same names, diversification can be thinner than it looks.

> [!key] Holding **many funds** is not the same as holding **many independent bets**. If your funds overlap on the same top stocks, you have concentration dressed up as diversification.

## Big idea

Industry money often clusters — in a few cities, in a few popular categories, and on the same marquee stocks. Portfolios *within* one AMC, or across the flexi-cap / focused / large-cap sleeves you own, can overlap heavily. The result: hidden single-stock risk.

## Why it matters

- **Herding** — concentrated inflows push the same names, inflating them together.
- **Hidden concentration** — three funds sharing a top-5 stock = one big position.
- **Correlations rise in stress** — crowding is fine *until* everyone exits at once, and the "diversified" holdings fall together.

> [!more] How to actually check overlap
> Most fund factsheets list top-10 holdings. Line up the top-10s of the equity funds you own; if the same 4–5 names dominate several of them, your true number of bets is smaller than your number of funds. Portfolio-overlap tools automate this.

## Investor move

- Check **top-holdings overlap** across your equity funds, not just their labels.
- Prefer **complementary mandates** over lookalikes with different names.
- Treat "popular / highly-rated" as a **risk lens**, not a badge of safety.
