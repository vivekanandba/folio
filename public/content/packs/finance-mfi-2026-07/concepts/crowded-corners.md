> When many funds and many cities chase the same names, diversification can be thinner than it looks.

## Big idea

Industry AUM often clusters in a handful of cities, and portfolios *within* an AMC (or across popular categories) can **overlap** heavily on the same stocks. “Many funds” does not automatically mean “many independent bets.”

## Why it matters

- Geographic concentration of inflows can amplify herding.
- Overlap across your holdings (flexi-cap + focused + large-cap from related sleeves) can create hidden single-stock risk.
- Crowding can be fine *until* exits scramble — then correlations rise.

## Investor move

- Check top holdings overlap across your equity funds.
- Prefer complementary mandates, not lookalike labels.
- Treat “popular” as a risk lens, not a badge of safety.
