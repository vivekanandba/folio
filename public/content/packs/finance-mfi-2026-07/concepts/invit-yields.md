# InvIT yields — headline vs pocket

> A startling yield is a clue to investigate structure, not a reason to skip reading.

## Big idea

Infrastructure Investment Trusts (InvITs) can advertise eye-catching annual yields. **Headline yield and money that actually hits your pocket are not the same**, because of trust structure, fees, asset life, payout composition, and unit-price risk.

## What you are buying

An InvIT owns income-producing infrastructure and passes cash to unitholders. Buying one is really three decisions: **asset class**, **counterparty**, and **concession profile** (terms and duration).

Illustrative buckets (India listed space):

- Transmission (long regulated tariffs)
- Roads (tolls or hybrid annuity contracts)
- Gas pipelines (tariffs vs a small set of shippers)

## Why yields mislead

- Payout may include return of capital, not only “income.”
- Assets and contracts age; cash flows are not a perpetual FD.
- Unit prices move — total return ≠ distribution yield alone.

## Investor move

Separate “looks like high income” marketing from “what I keep after structure and risk.”
